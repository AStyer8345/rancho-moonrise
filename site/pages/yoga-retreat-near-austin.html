Yoga Retreat Near Austin, Texas

April 24, 2026 · 5 min read

Most yoga retreats near Austin involve a long drive and a weekend of workshops in a conference-style lodge. Rancho Moonrise is a different kind of option — 36 acres of working ranch land 20 minutes from downtown, with monthly outdoor yoga events that run May through August and private retreat bookings available year-round.

The monthly series is called Yoga & Mimosas. It's a Saturday morning outdoor yoga session followed by brunch and pool access on the ranch. No trek to the Texas Hill Country. No three-hour drive. You're in your car for less than 30 minutes from most of Austin and you're on land where the only noise is ranch land noise.

The Yoga & Mimosas Events — What to Expect

Each Yoga & Mimosas event runs Saturday morning and is open to all skill levels. The yoga session is held outdoors on the ranch property — open space, central Texas air, no studio walls. After the session:

  • Mimosa brunch — food and drinks on property after the practice
  • Pool access — the resort-style pool is included so you can cool off and stay for the afternoon
  • Ranch setting — 36 acres with open pasture, fire pits, and the kind of space that's genuinely hard to find 20 minutes from a city this size

2026 Yoga & Mimosas dates:

  • Saturday, May 31
  • Saturday, June 28
  • Saturday, July 26
  • Saturday, August 30

Tickets sell in advance — check the RM Events page for availability and current pricing. These aren't massive productions. They sell out when people start booking May plans in April.

Staying Overnight — Turn It Into a Full Retreat Weekend

The public events are a few hours. But the ranch also has overnight accommodations — safari tents and cabins — so you can make a full retreat weekend out of it.

A Yoga & Mimosas weekend at Rancho Moonrise could look like:

  • Friday evening: Check in to a safari tent or cabin. Settle in, sit at the fire pit, decompress from the week. The Lodge is on property.
  • Saturday morning: Yoga session + mimosa brunch. Pool the rest of the afternoon.
  • Saturday evening: Dinner, fire pit, no alarm set for Sunday.
  • Sunday: Check out and drive back to Austin in under 30 minutes.

Overnight accommodations book through Cloudbeds separately from event tickets. If you want to stay the night before a Yoga & Mimosas event, book your room early — weekend dates fill before the event itself does.

Private Yoga Retreats — Booking the Ranch for Your Group

Beyond the public monthly events, Rancho Moonrise takes private bookings for yoga retreats. This works for:

  • Yoga studios running a student retreat or workshop day outside the studio
  • Bachelorette weekends where the bride wants a wellness-forward experience rather than a bar crawl — morning yoga, pool, overnight stay, actual sleep before the wedding month chaos
  • Corporate wellness — a half-day or full-day outdoor yoga session as part of a team retreat (pairs with the corporate retreat events program)
  • Friend group getaways — 10–20 people who want to do something intentional together without driving two hours

Private retreats can include outdoor yoga space, The Lodge bar, pool access, catering arrangements, and overnight accommodations for all guests. Inquiry goes through the private events team — not Cloudbeds. Have your guest count, preferred date, and whether you want overnight stays included when you reach out.

Why a Ranch Works Better Than a Studio for a Retreat

A studio retreat is a studio with a different schedule. A ranch retreat is an environment that does some of the work for you.

What the ranch setting adds that a rented studio or hotel conference room doesn't:

  • Outdoor space. Practicing outside on actual land is different from practicing near a window. No mirrors. No street noise. Central Texas morning air before the heat of the day.
  • No commute between activities. Yoga, brunch, pool, accommodations — everything is on the same 36 acres. You're not shuttling people around or coordinating Ubers between a studio, a restaurant, and a hotel.
  • The setting holds the experience. When people wake up in a safari tent on a ranch and do yoga outdoors and then swim in a pool and eat together, the setting is part of what makes it memorable. A conference hotel doesn't have that.
  • Easy drive from Austin. 20 minutes from downtown. If someone can't stay overnight, they can still show up for the morning and drive back. No one has to commit to a 3-hour drive for a half-day event.
