Glamping Near Austin, Texas — The Complete Guide

Published April 7, 2026 · 6 min read

Austin is one of the fastest-growing cities in the country, and it doesn't take long before the noise, traffic, and concrete start to wear on you. The good news: you don't need to drive four hours to feel like you've escaped. Twenty minutes east of downtown, Rancho Moonrise sits on 20 acres of open Texas ranch land in Manor, TX — and it's the closest full-service glamping destination to the city.

This guide covers everything you need to know about glamping near Austin: what it is, what to expect, what to pack, and how to make the most of your stay.

What Is Glamping?

Glamping — glamorous camping — is outdoor sleeping with indoor-level comfort. No tent poles. No sleeping bags. No rain fly disasters. At Rancho Moonrise, it looks like this: a safari-style canvas tent with a real queen bed, air conditioning, heat, a mini fridge, string lights, and a private fire pit outside your door. Or a hand-crafted cabin with floor-to-ceiling windows, a covered deck, and sweeping ranch views.

You're outdoors. You can hear the crickets, see the stars, and wake up with Hill Country air in your lungs. But you're also comfortable. That's the whole point.

How Far Is It From Austin?

Rancho Moonrise is located at 20117 Lockwood Road, Manor, TX 78653 — approximately 20 minutes east of downtown Austin via US-290 or TX-71. It's close enough to pop into Austin for dinner and still make it back for a fire under the stars. Most guests treat it as a full weekend stay rather than a day trip.

What's Available at Rancho Moonrise?

The property has 20 units split across two types of accommodations:

  • Safari Tents — Canvas-wall tents with queen, king, or bunk configurations. Tents #7–#12 share a communal bathhouse. Tents #13–#20 have private ensuite bathrooms. All tents include A/C, heat, mini fridge, and a fire pit.
  • Cabins — Hand-crafted wood cabins with queen beds, floor-to-ceiling windows, a private covered deck, and fire pit. Shared bathhouse access.

Both options are pet-friendly ($50 fee, one dog up to 60 lbs per reservation) and sleep 2–4 depending on configuration. The property holds up to 50 overnight guests across all units.

What's Included With Your Stay?

Every reservation includes access to the full ranch — no hidden fees for amenities. That means:

  • Resort-style pool with lounge chairs (open until 9 PM)
  • General Store on-site (beer, wine, snacks, essentials)
  • Complimentary WiFi in the Lodge
  • Golf cart service to and from your unit
  • Soap, shampoo, and conditioner in all accommodations
  • Fire pit at every site (firewood available for purchase)

The Neon Moon Bar & Lounge is open during events. The ranch also hosts live music, yoga sessions, crawfish boils, and seasonal celebrations throughout the year — check the events calendar before you book to time your stay with something happening on property.

What Should I Pack?

Less than you think. Linens, towels, toiletries, and a real bed are all provided. What you'll want to bring:

  • Comfortable clothes for outdoor temps (Texas evenings swing cooler than you expect)
  • Closed-toe shoes for walking the property
  • Bug spray — it's Texas
  • Swimsuit and sunscreen for the pool
  • One nice outfit if you're dining out in Austin
  • Phone charger (outlets are in every unit)

No outside alcohol is permitted on property. The General Store has you covered with beer and wine.

Check-In, Check-Out & Policies

Check-in is 4–8 PM daily. Check-out is 11 AM. Noise curfew is 9:30 PM across the property. Vehicles stay in the general lot overnight — not at your site. Golf cart service runs throughout the evening to shuttle you and your luggage.

Cancellation and pet policies are listed on the policies page.

When Should I Book?

Spring (March–May) and fall (September–November) are peak seasons near Austin. Weekends and event dates sell out weeks in advance. If you have a specific weekend in mind, book as early as possible — availability is real-time and there's no waitlist.

2026 Saturday dates are already filling. Weekdays and Sunday nights have more flexibility.
