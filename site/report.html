Your Website Is Losing Bookings Right Now

I ran a full conversion audit on the current site. The brand is strong — 4.9 stars, great photos, real demand. But the website isn't converting that demand into booked events. Here's why.

✓ 3 of 5 Gaps Already Fixed

The new website now has self-service tour scheduling, an AI chatbot answering questions 24/7, and a smart contact page that routes each visitor to the right experience. Two remaining gaps (auto-response emails and pricing transparency) are up next.

Conversion Gap 1 — ✓ FIXED

No Way to Schedule a Venue Tour

Wedding couples rank venue tours as the most important step before booking. They had to fill out a form and wait for a callback.

✓ Done: Calendly "Schedule a Venue Tour" and "Virtual Tour" buttons are now on the Weddings page, Private Events page, and Contact page. Couples book directly — no phone tag required.
Conversion Gap 2 — ✓ FIXED

Chat Widget Goes Dark After Hours

The old chat widget sat idle unless manually staffed. Couples browsing at 9 PM got no response.

✓ Done: An AI-powered chatbot trained on Rancho Moonrise content is now live on every page — answers questions about packages, pricing, capacity, policies, and amenities 24/7. When it can't answer, it routes to the inquiry form or phone. See it in action →
Conversion Gap 3 — High Impact

No Instant Response When Someone Inquires

When a couple fills out the wedding inquiry form, they get... nothing. No confirmation email, no package info, no next steps. They're left wondering if anyone even received it. Venues that respond within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to book the lead.

Fix: I'll build an automated email sequence — instant confirmation with a Wedding Package PDF, Calendly link, and photo gallery. If they don't respond in 48 hours, a follow-up with testimonials. Then another at day 5. All automatic.
Conversion Gap 4 — High Impact

No Pricing Info on the Weddings Page

Budget-conscious couples — a huge portion of the market — will not fill out an inquiry form without some idea of pricing. No range, no package teaser, nothing. They leave and find a competitor who's upfront about costs.

Fix: I'll add package teasers with starting prices, a downloadable Wedding Guide PDF (which also captures their email), and urgency messaging like "2026 Saturdays booking fast." Even a range like "Packages starting at $X" dramatically reduces drop-off.
Conversion Gap 5 — ✓ FIXED

Contact Page Treats Everyone the Same

A wedding couple, a corporate event planner, and someone asking about pool passes all saw the same generic form.

✓ Done: The Contact page now has a segmented flow — "What brings you to Rancho Moonrise?" routes to 5 tailored paths: Wedding (with partner name, date range, guest count), Private Event, Accommodation (→ Cloudbeds), Pool Pass (→ ResortPass), and General Question.
