## Executive Summary

The Austin glamping and wedding-venue SERP continues to evolve. Since the April 6 baseline, three new competitors have entered the top results for "glamping near austin": **Udoscape** (eco-pods on Lake Travis), **Camposanto ATX** (yurts on Lake Travis), and **Safari for the Soul** (adult-only riverfront safari tents in Spicewood). Most notably, **Lucky Arrow Retreat has added safari tents** as a new accommodation type — directly encroaching on what had been a Rancho Moonrise differentiator. The pool day pass SERP is now dominated by ResortPass-listed hotel properties downtown; Lucky Arrow is the only non-hotel resort appearing on ResortPass for Austin.

Rancho Moonrise remains absent from organic results for all 14 target keywords. The DNS cutover to the new Vercel site remains the single largest unlock — no SEO work can register until that happens.

**⚡ Top 3 Actionable Findings (Week of April 13):**

1. **⚡ "Safari for the Soul" now directly owns the "safari tent austin" keyword brand.** A new property (safariforthesoulglamping.com) with "safari" in the brand name is ranking for this term. Combined with Lucky Arrow adding safari tents, Rancho Moonrise's window to own "safari tent near Austin" as a keyword is closing. A dedicated landing page needs to ship before DNS cutover.

2. **⚡ Lucky Arrow Retreat added safari tents as a new accommodation type.** Their newest addition features a private wood deck, s'mores kit, and private chiminea. This directly narrows what had been a differentiation gap. Rancho Moonrise must respond by emphasizing scale (50+ overnight guests, full Event Barn, pool, weddings for 300+) — things Lucky Arrow cannot match at their 40-room property.

3. **⚡ Three new glamping competitors entered the Austin SERP.** Udoscape (Lago Vista pods), Camposanto ATX (Lake Travis yurts), and Cameron Ranch Glamping (Lake Bastrop mirror houses) are now indexing on aggregators and booking platforms. The Austin glamping market is adding supply faster than Rancho Moonrise is adding search presence. Glamping Hub listing is still absent — this gap is now more costly than it was in April.

## Section 2 — Competitor Highlights

### New Entrants Since April 6 Baseline

#### Udoscape Eco-Glamping Resort — [udoscaperesorts.com](https://udoscaperesorts.com)
**Location:** Lago Vista, TX (Lake Travis area, ~45 min from Austin)  
**What they have:** 11 luxury glamping pods (Deluxe to Crown-Lux tiers), panoramic Hill Country views, eco-focused positioning. Indexed on Booking.com (64 reviews), TripAdvisor, Airbnb.  
**Why it matters:** Now appearing in "glamping near austin tx" top results. Has meaningful review volume on booking platforms that Rancho Moonrise lacks. Eco/sustainability angle differentiates from Rancho Moonrise's event-ranch positioning.

#### Camposanto ATX — [camposantoatx.com](https://camposantoatx.com)
**Location:** Lakeway/Travis County (Lake Travis, private neighborhood "Graveyard Point")  
**What they have:** 2 luxury yurts (The Peacock, The Macaw) on Lake Travis. Indexed on Expedia, Hotels.com, TripAdvisor 2026.  
**Why it matters:** Very small (2 yurts) but has Lake Travis as a differentiator. Niche positioning — not a direct competitor for weddings/events, but competes for glamping-discovery traffic.

#### Cameron Ranch Glamping — [cameronranchglamping.com](https://cameronranchglamping.com)
**Location:** Lake Bastrop, ~1 hour from Austin  
**What they have:** "Mirror Houses" with mirrored exteriors reflecting surrounding forest/lake; hotel-quality bedding, glass walls, full HVAC. Published a blog post ("Best Glamping Near Austin Texas") ranking for the target keyword.  
**Why it matters:** Their own blog is ranking organically for "best glamping near austin" — meaning a competitor is capturing keyword traffic via content, not just aggregator listings.

#### Safari for the Soul — [safariforthesoulglamping.com](https://safariforthesoulglamping.com)
**Location:** Spicewood and Marble Falls (~45-50 min from Austin)  
**What they have:** 4 AC/heated luxury safari tents, adult-only, pet-friendly, riverfront. Private bathrooms and hot tubs. No shared amenities. Expedia listing.  
**Why it matters:** Brand name contains "safari" — directly competing for the "safari tent austin" keyword Rancho Moonrise was positioned to own. Their domain/brand is optimized for that query in a way Rancho Moonrise's current URL structure is not.

## Section 4 — Quick Wins This Week

1. **Create a dedicated safari tent landing page.** Safari for the Soul now owns the "safari tent + brand name" angle. Rancho Moonrise needs to counter with a deeper content page — `/safari-tents-austin/` — that emphasizes proximity (20 min from Austin, not 45-50), scale (multi-tent for groups, not adult-only-only), and the combined resort experience (pool, bar, events). Ship before DNS cutover so it indexes on day one.

2. **Submit to Glamping Hub.** Three new properties are now indexed on Glamping Hub that weren't appearing in April. Every week the listing is absent, new entrants with fewer stays and reviews are gaining discovery advantage. This is a free submission. Assign to Adam or Ashley — 15 minutes.

3. **Add Lucky Arrow-style corporate retreat package page.** A blog post is not enough — Lucky Arrow has two dedicated pages with package descriptions appearing in corporate retreat search results. Convert `pages/host-your-event.html` or add `/corporate-retreat/` with specific pricing tiers, group capacity numbers, and team-building activity list.
