SEO/AEOSearch & AI Visibility — Ongoing

Starts immediately · Ongoing weekly · Accelerates after DNS cutover

Rancho Moonrise does NOT rank for any non-brand search query. "Best glamping near Austin," "wedding venues Austin TX ranch," "pool day pass Austin" — zero presence. AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) don't cite Rancho Moonrise at all. The Vercel site has good schema and page structure — but it's not live yet. Launching the new site on the main domain is the single biggest SEO/AEO unlock.

S1Split blog posts into individual pages with unique URLs
ClaudeAUTOSEO: F → C

All 6 blog posts live on a single blog.html page — Google can't index them individually. Each post needs its own HTML file in site/blog/ with Article schema, canonical URL, meta description, and links back to the blog index. Blog index gets CollectionPage schema. This is a prerequisite for any blog SEO.

S2Add answer-first H2 summaries to key pages
ClaudeAUTOAEO

AI engines extract the first sentence after each H2. Currently, page content buries the answer. Add a direct, extractable answer in the first sentence of every H2 section on: glamping page, weddings page, corporate retreat page, accommodations page. Example: "Rancho Moonrise is a 34-acre glamping ranch 20 minutes from downtown Austin with luxury safari tents, cabins, a pool, and event spaces for up to 200 guests."

S3Build topical authority clusters with internal linking
ClaudeAUTOSEO + AEO

Pages exist but don't interlink deeply. Build three clusters: (1) Glamping cluster: glamping page ↔ packing guide ↔ glamping vs camping ↔ Austin outdoor activities, (2) Wedding cluster: weddings ↔ ranch wedding tips ↔ wedding venues near Austin ↔ bachelorette, (3) Events cluster: events ↔ corporate retreats ↔ host your event ↔ things to do Manor TX. Each page should link to 3-5 related pages with contextual anchor text.

S4Add BreadcrumbList + SpeakableSpecification schema
ClaudeAUTOAEO

Add BreadcrumbList schema to all subpages (Home > Glamping > ...). Add SpeakableSpecification to homepage, glamping, weddings, and corporate pages — this tells AI engines exactly which text blocks are quotable answers. Also add AggregateRating schema pulling from the 125 Google reviews (4.9★).

S5Create "Pool Day Pass Austin" dedicated page
ClaudeAUTOSEO + Revenue

ResortPass already ranks for "pool day pass Austin" with a Rancho Moonrise listing. Own this query directly with a dedicated page on the new site — pricing, hours (10AM-6PM), what's included, photos, link to ResortPass booking. This is high-margin, low-effort revenue with search demand.

AdamClaudeManualSEO: F → B

After DNS cutover: (1) Set up Google Search Console for ranchomoonrise.com, (2) Submit XML sitemap, (3) Request indexing of all key pages, (4) Set up redirect map from old BofillTech URLs, (5) Monitor indexing weekly — all pages should be indexed within 14 days. This is the condensed, scannable version of the full voice guide on GitHub. Use it when writing anything that'll be seen by a guest, bride, event planner, or follower — blog posts, captions, emails, GBP descriptions, ad copy. The full guide lives at VOICE-GUIDE.md.

Who We Are (in one breath)

Rancho Moonrise is a glamping and events ranch on 20 acres in Manor, TX — 20 minutes from downtown Austin. Luxury safari tents, hand-crafted cabins, destination weddings, live music, and a pool you won't want to leave. Family-friendly. Pet-friendly. Unforgettable.

Voice in Three Words

WarmWe talk like a friend who owns a ranch, not a hotel chain. Welcoming without being performative.
GroundedWe're real. Concrete details, actual numbers, honest descriptions. No fluff, no overselling.
MagneticThe writing should pull people in. Sensory language, short punchy lines, and a "you need to be here" energy.

Things We Never Say

AvoidWhySay Instead
"Rustic chic"Overused, means nothing"hand-crafted cabins with floor-to-ceiling windows"
"Nestled in"Every venue says this"Located in Manor, TX" or "on 20 acres in..."
"Curated experience"Corporate hospitality speak"An experience you won't forget"
"World-class"Unearned superlativeUse real proof: "4.9 stars, 127 reviews"
"Bespoke"Pretentious for a Texas ranch"Custom" or "tailored to your group"
"Amenities include..."Reads like a hotel listingIntegrate features naturally
"Inquire within"Formal, creates friction"Send us a message" or "Call 737-291-1260"
"Limited availability!" ⚠Desperate urgency kills trust"2026 dates are booking fast — limited Saturday availability."

Writing Patterns That Work

Short + punchy for features.

A/C, heat, private fire pits — glamping without compromise.

Em dashes for rhythm.

Luxury safari tents, hand-crafted cabins, destination weddings, and unforgettable events — all on 20 acres of Texas Hill Country.

Second person — make the reader the guest.

"The ranch is entirely yours." / "Walk back to your tent under the stars."

Concrete numbers over vague claims.

"200+ guests" not "large parties welcome." "20 minutes from downtown Austin" not "conveniently located."

Sensory details that put people on the property.

"under the wide Texas sky" / "oak-shaded pastures with sweeping Hill Country views" / "campfire nights and starry skies"

Instagram Voice (Separate From Website)

Instagram is more casual and emoji-forward than the website. Two caption styles — short-vibes and event-promo. Always tag collaborators. Almost no hashtags. Exclamation points are welcome here (but not on the website).

Style 1 — Short & Vibes: Hook line + one detail + emoji + soft CTA.

Meet you at Rancho! Our tents come in a variety of options, many including full bathrooms. Come see for yourself 🤩

Style 2 — Event Promo: Emoji + event name + date → 1–2 sentence description → practical details → "link in bio 🎟️" → tag everyone involved.

@martabandofficial LIVE IN AUSTIN, TX @rancho_moonrise MARCH 28!! 🤩 Music all night long featuring @monicawall (7PM), @martabandofficial (8PM) followed by a late night dj!! 🎶🍹🎧 Come early for $5 margs at the pool & stay for a show! Tickets linked in bio 🎟️

What NOT to post: Generic "another beautiful day at the ranch" captions, hashtag dumps (#glamping #austin #blessed), corporate-polish language, captions longer than the event warrants, posts without tagging partners.
