What's Broken (And What It's Costing You)

The "COMING SOON!" Problem
When someone Googles "Rancho Moonrise," the description Google shows beneath your link still says "COMING SOON!" — signaling to every potential guest that you might not be open. That's a 15-minute fix that's been costing you clicks for over a year.

You're invisible for every keyword that matters. Search "glamping austin texas" — you're not there. "Wedding venue manor texas" — not there. "Corporate retreat near austin" — not there. These are the exact searches your highest-intent customers are typing. They're finding your competitors instead.

Here's why:

  • Every page on your site has nearly the same title tag — Google can't tell your wedding page from your accommodations page
  • None of your images have alt text — Google literally can't see your property photos
  • Your homepage has duplicate H1 headers (both just say "Rancho moonrise") instead of telling Google what you do
  • No structured data markup telling Google "this is a lodging business with these amenities, this rating, these prices"
  • No blog or content strategy — zero informational pages to capture top-of-funnel searches

Your Google Business Profile Is Running on Autopilot

Your GBP has 122 reviews at 4.9 stars — that's exceptional. But the profile itself appears to have been auto-created by OTAs like Expedia. There's:

  • No business description at all — the single most impactful field for local search is completely empty
  • No business hours set
  • No direct booking link — guests are being funneled through Expedia/Hotels.com (which charges you 15-20% commission)
  • Category set to "Hotel" instead of Resort, Wedding Venue, Glamping Site, Event Venue

The Google Local Pack (the map results at the top of search) is where 46% of local searches end. With 122 five-star reviews, you should own that space for glamping and event queries in Austin. Right now, you're not even in the game.
