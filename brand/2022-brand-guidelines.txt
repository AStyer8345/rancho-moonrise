RANCHO MOONRISE PG. 02 BRAND GUIDELINES

PRIMARY LOGO The logo is the established look and feel of the Rancho Moonrise brand — a visual element to shout to the world who we are and what we're all about. The logo evokes a laid back cosmic cowboy aesthetic while visually communicating our surrounding landscapes in order to communicate to our patrons what they can expect when on our property.

The primary logo should be the first one you reach for when communicating the Rancho Moonrise brand, particular to outside vendors or external communication.

All logos are available in both a Textured and Non-Textured effect. Choose which one to use based on application and output.

RANCHO MOONRISE PG. 03 BRAND GUIDELINES LOGOS

SECONDARY Rancho Moonrise's secondary logo is identical to the primary logo, with some variation. The location flank text has been removed and the symbols in the flanks increased in size. Grab this logo when you don't need to communicate the location, or the location is written elsewhere.
TERTIARY

Rancho Moonrise's tertiary logo omits any supporting text and relies solely on symbols to support the name. Grab this logo when you want something simple, or when you're using a lot of text elsewhere.

PG. 06
CLEAR SPACE Allow the logo and monogram to breath and remain clearly seen by following the recommended clear space rules.

The logo uses the size of the second "o" in Moonrise. The monogram utilizes the moon to determine the clear space.

MINIMUM SIZE To ensure clear visibility of the logo when at smaller sizes it's important to produce the logo no smaller that 1 inch square in print, and 72px on screen. For the monogram, go no smaller than 1/2 inch square in print, and 36px on screen.

RANCHO MOONRISE PG. 22 BRAND GUIDELINES

VOICE & TONE The Rancho Moonrise brand goes far beyond 1. BE AUTHENTIC just the visual aesthetics. It's also about the language used and voice we put forward. Here are some key tips to keep in mind when verbally communicating about the brand.

Speak with ease, friendliness, and approachability. Imagine you're having a conversation with someone you've known for ages and have invited over to your place for a chill hang.

2.

BE A LITTLE TEXAN Sprinkle in words like "howdy" and "y'all" every now and then. Keep it authentic though and don't overuse these. You want it to feel real — not forced.

3.

BE NATURAL Find ways to playfully and causally speak about the landscape when possible, as it's part of the draw to Rancho Moonrise. Take pride in the area and share the knowledge of the history and natural wonders it provides. No one likes a know it all though, so keep it laid back.
