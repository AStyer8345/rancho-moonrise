VISUAL DESIGN LANGUAGE (extracted from all boards)
============================================================

PRIMARY AESTHETIC: Vintage/Retro Poster Art
The dominant visual language across all boards is 1960s-1970s poster art
with hand-drawn illustration. This is NOT a slick, corporate, or minimalist
look. It is warm, tactile, and intentionally imperfect — like something
you'd find at an Austin flea market or a vintage print shop.

TYPOGRAPHY PATTERNS:
- Hand-lettered headlines (not clean sans-serif)
- Vintage display fonts — slab serifs, decorative, sometimes psychedelic
- Mixed-weight compositions — big bold event name, smaller details below
- Occasional all-caps block lettering for dates and locations
- Retro script fonts for softer events (yoga, brunch, sound bath)
- NEVER: corporate sans-serif, tech-startup clean, generic Canva templates

COLOR PALETTE (recurring across boards):
- Terracotta / Burnt Orange — the anchor color, earthy and warm
- Olive Green / Sage — natural, botanical, grounding
- Dusty Rose / Coral Pink — soft warmth, feminine without being precious
- Turquoise / Teal — accent pops, desert sky references
- Deep Brown / Chocolate — grounding, ranch heritage
- Cream / Off-White — warm paper/canvas texture backgrounds
- Midnight Blue / Navy — evening event sophistication
- Sunshine Yellow / Mustard — energy, warmth, Texas sun
- NEVER: neon, pure white backgrounds, corporate blue, sterile gray

ILLUSTRATION STYLE:
- Hand-drawn botanical illustrations (leaves, flowers, cacti)
- Woodcut / linocut print aesthetic
- Folk art influences — Mexican folk art, Texas western art
- Vintage travel poster compositions
- Collage-style layouts mixing illustration with photography
- Animal illustrations (horses, dogs) in retro style
- Sun/moon/celestial motifs
- NEVER: stock photography, AI-generated slick imagery, clip art

LAYOUT & COMPOSITION:
- Event poster format — clear hierarchy: event name → date → details
- Centered compositions with decorative borders
- Layered collage arrangements for mood boards
- Strong use of negative space with warm background colors
- Hand-drawn frames and ornamental dividers
- Vintage ticket/playbill formatting for entertainment events

TEXTURE & FINISH:
- Paper grain / canvas texture — looks printed, not digital
- Slightly faded/aged look — not crisp and clean
- Matte finish feel — nothing glossy or metallic
- Ink bleed / imperfect edges — intentional roughness
- Risograph / screen-print inspired color overlays

BRAND VOICE IMPLICATIONS (from visual patterns)
============================================================

The Pinterest boards reveal a brand that wants to feel like:
✓ A cool friend's ranch party invitation, not a corporate event listing
✓ Something you'd pin to a corkboard, not scroll past on Instagram
✓ Handmade and personal, like a local artist designed it
✓ Austin weird meets Texas ranch heritage
✓ Wellness without being precious — yoga with mimosas, not silent meditation
✓ Cultural and artistic — references to Marfa, Mexican Modern art, folk traditions
✓ Nostalgic but not dated — vintage-inspired for a modern audience

The visual identity tells you this is NOT:
✗ A luxury resort (too corporate, too polished)
✗ A wellness retreat center (too serious, too clean)
✗ A generic event venue (too bland, no personality)
✗ A tech-forward glamping startup (too sleek, too minimal)

It IS:
→ A creative, artsy Texas ranch that throws events your cool friends invite you to
→ Where the flyer itself makes you want to go
→ Where the design feels like the experience — warm, handmade, memorable

DESIGN DO'S AND DON'TS (distilled from Pinterest curation)
============================================================

DO:
- Use hand-drawn or vintage-style illustrations
- Choose warm, earthy color palettes with bold accent pops
- Use display/decorative fonts for headlines — personality matters
- Add paper texture or grain to backgrounds
- Reference vintage poster art and mid-century design
- Make flyers that feel collectible — worth pinning to a wall
- Mix western/ranch motifs with wellness/artisan culture
- Keep it fun, warm, and slightly imperfect

DON'T:
- Use stock photography as the primary visual
- Default to Canva templates with generic layouts
- Use corporate sans-serif fonts (Helvetica, Arial, etc.)
- Make anything that looks like it came from a hotel chain
- Use pure white backgrounds — always warm it up
- Over-polish — some roughness and texture is the point
- Use neon colors or high-contrast digital gradients
- Make it look "techy" or "startup-y"
