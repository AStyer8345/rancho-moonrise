## Who We Are (in one breath)

Rancho Moonrise is a glamping and events ranch on 36 acres, 20 minutes from downtown Austin. Safari tents, hand-crafted cabins, destination weddings, live music, donkeys and ranch life, and a pool you won't want to leave. Family-friendly. Pet-friendly. Unforgettable.

---

## Voice in Three Words

**Warm. Grounded. Magnetic.**

- **Warm** — We talk like a friend who owns a ranch, not a hotel chain. Welcoming without being performative. Texturey, a little country twang — organic and handmade, never slick and corporate.
- **Grounded** — We're real. Concrete details, actual numbers, honest descriptions. No fluff, no overselling. "Just show up" is the energy — real beds, no gear required.
- **Magnetic** — The writing should pull people in. Sensory language, short punchy lines, and a "you need to be here" energy. Lean into the animal life — donkeys, ranch vibes — people love it.

## Writing Patterns We Use

### Short + punchy for features
> A/C, heat, real beds, fire pits throughout the property — glamping without compromise.

### Em dashes for rhythm
> Safari tents, hand-crafted cabins, destination weddings, and unforgettable events — all on 36 acres, 20 minutes from downtown Austin.

### Second person — make the reader the guest
> "The ranch is entirely yours."
> "Walk back to your tent under the stars."
> "Your own private retreat."

### Concrete numbers over vague claims
> "200+ guests" not "large parties welcome"
> "20 minutes from downtown Austin" not "conveniently located"
> "36 acres" not "a huge property"
> Do NOT list a unit/room count — inventory fluctuates (15, 18, 19). Use "safari tents and cabins" or "overnight accommodations" instead.

### Sensory details — put people on the property
> "under the wide Texas sky"
> "beneath the moonlit Texas sky"
> "campfire nights and starry skies"
> "golden Texas sunset"
> "donkeys at the fence line"
> "pool bar open, music in the air"

**NEVER use:** "Hill Country" (factually wrong — we're not in the Hill Country, locals will call it out), "oak-shaded" / "under the oaks" (no oak trees on the property).

### Conversational asides that show personality
> "The gift she actually wants."
> "Stay connected while you disconnect."
> "So you don't have to leave the ranch."
> "The perfect Saturday morning."

## Things We Never Say

| Avoid | Why | Say Instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| **"Luxury"** | Not the brand — Ashley's direction. Organic/handmade, not aspirational-hotel. | Describe the actual thing: "hand-crafted cabins", "real beds", "A/C + heat" |
| **"Texas Hill Country"** | Factually wrong — Rancho Moonrise is NOT in the Hill Country. Locals will call it out. | "20 minutes from downtown Austin" or "Central Texas" |
| **"Manor, TX" / "Mainer"** | Ashley's rule — always anchor to Austin, not Manor. | "20 minutes from downtown Austin" |
| **"General Store"** | Renamed. | "The Lodge" |
| **"Neon Moon Barn Lounge" as a walk-in bar** | The Neon Moon Barn Lounge is EVENT-ONLY, not open to walk-in guests. Creates confusion. | Only reference as a private event space, or remove entirely |
| **"Full-service bar open daily"** | False — bars are event-only. | "Drinks available at the Lodge" or "margaritas, beer, wine, and more at the pool bar and Lodge" |
| **"Yoga under the oaks"** | No oak trees on the property. | "Yoga al fresco" or "poolside yoga" |
| **"4 ceremony sites"** | Misleading — guests can get married anywhere (pool, grass, open field). | "Unlimited options for your own unique layout" |
| **"Private fire pit at every cabin/tent"** | Not every unit has a private fire pit. | "Fire pits throughout the property" |
| **"20 units, sleeps ~50" or any specific unit count** | Inventory fluctuates (15 / 18 / 19). Any static number will go stale. | "Safari tents and cabins" or "overnight accommodations" |
| "Rustic chic" | Overused, means nothing | Describe the actual thing: "hand-crafted cabins with floor-to-ceiling windows" |
| "Nestled in" | Every venue says this | "20 minutes from downtown Austin" or "on 36 acres..." |
| "Curated experience" | Corporate hospitality speak | "An experience you won't forget" or just describe what happens |
| "World-class" | Unearned superlative | Use real proof: "4.9 stars, 125 reviews" |
| "Bespoke" | Pretentious for a Texas ranch | "Custom" or "tailored to your group" |
| "Glamorous camping" | Explaining the word defeats the vibe | Just say "glamping" — people know |
| "Amenities include..." | Reads like a hotel listing | Use short feature lists or integrate naturally |
| "Inquire within" | Formal, creates friction | "Send us a message" or "Call 737-291-1260" |
| "Starting at $X" | Prices aren't public — tours required | "Schedule a tour to see packages" |
| "View Wedding Packages" (CTA) | Wedding packages are NOT listed online — tour required | "Learn More" → wedding page |
| "Limited availability!" with exclamation | Desperate urgency kills trust | "2026 dates are booking fast — only limited Saturday availability remaining." (factual urgency) |

## Audience Personas (write for these people)

### 1. The Couple (Weddings)
- Engaged, 28-38, Austin-area or destination
- Wants something unique — not another hotel ballroom
- Cares about: exclusivity, photos, overnight accommodations for guests, vibe
- Pain point: venue shopping fatigue, sticker shock, complicated vendor coordination
- **Speak to:** "The entire ranch is yours. No outside guests."

### 2. The Weekend Escaper (Glamping)
- 25-45, Austin metro, looking for a getaway without a 4-hour drive
- Wants: disconnect from the city, Instagram-worthy, comfortable
- Pain point: "real" camping is too rough, hotels are boring
- **Speak to:** "Glamping without compromise — A/C, heat, and your own fire pit."

### 3. The Event Planner (Corporate / Private)
- Planning for a group — corporate retreat, birthday, reunion
- Wants: unique venue, easy logistics, flexible space
- Pain point: boring conference rooms, hard-to-coordinate offsite logistics
- **Speak to:** "From 20 to 200 — we've hosted it all."

### 4. The Fun-Seeker (Public Events)
- 25-40, Austin social scene, looking for something different this weekend
- Wants: live music, pool, vibes, something to post about
- Pain point: same bars, same shows, nothing new
- **Speak to:** "Crawfish, cold drinks, and live music under the stars. A Texas night to remember."

## Social Media Voice (from actual @rancho_moonrise Instagram)

### Instagram — The Real Voice

The Instagram voice is **more casual and emoji-forward** than the website. Two distinct caption styles:

#### Style 1: Short & Vibes (non-event posts)
Ultra-short captions (1-2 sentences) that let the photo do the talking. Conversational, exclamation points welcome, emoji as punctuation.

**Real examples from the account:**
> Meet you at Rancho! Our tents come in a variety of options, many including full bathrooms. Come see for yourself 🤩

> Robes are our specialty! 😍🧖 Rooms available to stay this weekend… link in bio!

**Pattern:** Hook line + one detail + emoji + soft CTA ("link in bio", "come see for yourself")

#### Style 2: Event Promo (structured)
Follows a clear formula: hook → description → schedule details → CTA. Emoji used as section markers. Tags collaborators liberally.

**Real examples from the account:**
> @martabandofficial LIVE IN AUSTIN, TX @rancho_moonrise MARCH 28!! 🤩 Music all night long featuring @monicawall (7PM), @martabandofficial (8PM) followed by a late night dj!! 🎶🍹🎧 Come early for $5 margs at the pool & stay for a show! Tickets linked in bio🎟️ | Overnight accommodations available!

> Sunday 2/22! Sun's out! Enjoy your day with a high-energy yoga x pilates led by @well_withelle 💪😎 Stick around after the class to enjoy our hot tub, lounge pool and complimentary bev! 🧘💚🧘‍♀️🧘

> Drift into relaxation under the full moon during our Floating Sound Bath led by @rebel_yogi_life on April 1st! 🌕✨ Crystal bowls, chimes and soothing sounds calm you and welcome in a new lunar cycle. Come rest, reset, and realign.

> 🐰 Rancho Moonrise Easter Extravaganza: Egg Hunt & Brunch! April 5th @ 10AM 🌷 [description] · 🥚 Easter Egg Hunt: [details] · 🛍️ Vendor Market: [details]

**Event promo pattern:**
1. Emoji + event name + date/time
2. 1-2 sentence description (energetic, sensory)
3. Practical details (schedule, check-in, what's included)
4. "Tickets linked in bio 🎟️" or "Reserve your spot through the link in our bio! ✨"
5. Tag all collaborators/performers/photographers

### Instagram Formatting Rules (from actual usage)
- **Emojis:** 2-5 per post. Used as punctuation and section dividers, not decoration
- **Hashtags:** Almost none in recent posts — algorithm-driven, not hashtag-driven
- **Tagging:** Always tag performers, partners, photographers (@capturingcarrphotography, @rebel_yogi_life, etc.)
- **Exclamation points:** Used freely — this is NOT the restrained website voice
- **"Link in bio":** Standard CTA, always present on event/booking posts
- **Double exclamation:** OK for hype moments ("MARCH 28!!")
- **Recurring phrases:** "Meet you at Rancho!", "Come see for yourself", "Stick around after..."
- **No long paragraphs** — even event posts stay scannable

### Instagram — What NOT to Post
- Generic stock-photo-energy captions ("Another beautiful day at the ranch!")
- Hashtag dumps (#glamping #austin #texas #ranch #wedding #travel #blessed)
- Overly polished/corporate language
- Captions longer than the event itself warrants
- Posts without tagging relevant people/partners

### Facebook
- Same voice as Instagram but slightly more detailed captions (Facebook audience reads more)
- Event posts can include full schedule/details
- OK to share community comments, guest stories, local partnerships
- Share Instagram content but add context for the Facebook audience

### TikTok
- Casual, behind-the-scenes energy
- Show the real ranch — setup, staff, animals, golden hour, event prep
- Trending sounds OK if they fit naturally
- Don't try too hard — authenticity over production value
- The account uses Reels on IG already — same energy for TikTok

### LinkedIn
- Professional but still warm — this is the corporate retreat / event planner audience
- Focus on the business case: team building, offsite retreats, unique venue
- OK to share milestones, press, partnerships (Lone Star Beer, Brides of Austin, etc.)

## Blog Voice

- Conversational and informative — like a friend who knows Austin inside and out
- First person plural ("we") or second person ("you")
- Lead with what the reader wants to know, not what we want to say
- Include practical details: directions, what to bring, what to expect
- Naturally weave in location keywords (Manor TX, near Austin, Central Texas)
- Every post should have a clear CTA — book a stay, check events, schedule a tour
- Avoid listicle-farm energy ("Top 10 Reasons To...")  — write with a point of view

---

## Email Voice

- Subject lines: short, curiosity-driven, no ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation
- Body: warm, scannable, one clear CTA per email
- Sign off as "The Rancho Moonrise Team" or "— Ashley & the RM Team"
- Unsubscribe language should be friendly, not passive-aggressive

**Good subject lines:**
> New event just dropped
> Your Saturday plan (sorted)
> Tents are filling up for May

**Bad subject lines:**
> DON'T MISS OUT!!! Upcoming Events at Rancho Moonrise!!!
> Newsletter #47 — April Updates

---

## Visual + Copy Pairing Notes

- **Hero sections:** Big evocative image + short aspirational headline + one supporting line + CTA buttons
- **Feature cards:** Image + 2-line description. Don't overwrite.
- **Testimonials:** Real quotes, attributed to platform ("— Google Review, 5 Stars"). No fake names.
- **Event cards:** Date prominently displayed, 1-2 sentence description, clear ticket/RSVP link
- **CTAs:** Always a verb first — "Book Your Stay", "Schedule a Tour", "Learn More" (for wedding page — never "View Wedding Packages" since packages aren't public), "Get Tickets"
