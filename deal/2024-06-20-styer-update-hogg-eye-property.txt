Hi Nance and Donna,

I wanted to give you an update on where we are at with the purchase of the 10 acre property off of Hogg Eye. It has been moving much more slowly as I (or anyone) would like but there has been a lot of back and forth between the sellers and Christopher.

To summarize, Christopher submitted an offer months ago. The offer included basically a full price offer but with a closing date of 12 months out. This would allow us to go under contract, put in earnest money, then we would conduct due diligence/feasibility and get permitting completed. Once that was complete, we would then close on the property. If we run into any major issues we could terminate the contract and get the earnest money back and go on our way.

The sellers have made clear they are not willing to do this which I believe is understandable. They don't want to lock the property up and take it off the market for up to a year and potentially wind up with nothing.

They are proposing a 4 to 6 month closing timeline and then are willing to give us the option to extend for a monthly fee. That fee has not been determined yet but will likely be $3k to $5k for each 30 day extension.

Christopher recently spoke with his business partners regarding the property, and they have proposed the following plan:

1. Christopher and his team are willing to cover all engineering costs and other fees related to the permitting and zoning of the property. He estimates these costs to be between $45,000 and $75,000.
2. They propose that Rancho Moonrise/you cover the earnest money, option fee, and extension fees.

Here are the specifics of the proposal:
- Earnest Money: $10,000 to $15,000
- Extension Fees: We would aim to get 4 to 6 months before having to pay extension fees every month. These fees are expected to be $3,000 to $5,000 per month.
  - It's possible we might have to pay up to 12 months of extension fees, but likely around 6 months.
  - We would negotiate for these fees to be credited to the purchase at closing.
  - At closing, Christopher mentioned we could work out a plan where all the money paid by Rancho Moonrise/you could be reimbursed.

Risks and Probabilities:
- The primary risk is that if we do not get the approval, we would lose the funds completely.
- Christopher believes there is an 80% to 90% probability that we will eventually get approval.

Timeline and Unknowns:
- There's a possibility that everything could be ready to go in 6 months, potentially resulting in minimal or no extension fees.
- However, there's also a chance the process could take up to 18 months.

Summary of Proposal:
- Christopher's Team: Cover all engineering costs and permitting/zoning fees ($45,000 to $75,000).
- Rancho Moonrise: Earnest Money: $10,000 to $15,000. Extension Fees: $3,000 to $5,000 per month.
- Risks: Losing funds if approval is not obtained. 80% to 90% probability of eventual approval.
- Timeline: Potentially ready in 6 months. Process could take up to 18 months.
